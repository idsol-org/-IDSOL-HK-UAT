FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) 2024?

The International Data Science Olympiad 2024 is a global competition designed to challenge and inspire the brightest minds in data science. It brings together students, professionals, and data enthusiasts from around the world to solve real-world problems using data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. The Olympiad provides a platform to showcase your analytical skills, creativity, and innovation in data science, with participants competing for prestigious titles, awards, and global recognition.

The IDSOL Finals is an annual event that brings hundreds of innovative students and professionals together. The mixing produces magical moments of inspiration and discussion, leading to further collaboration and possibly new projects and ventures.

Who can participate in the IDSOL 2024?

The Olympiad is open to individuals and teams from all educational backgrounds and professional levels. Whether you are a high school student, university student, or a working professional in the field of data science, statistics, computer science, or related disciplines, you are eligible to participate. The competition is designed to accommodate various levels of expertise, from beginners to seasoned data scientists.

Are there any prerequisites to join the competition?

While there are no strict prerequisites for joining the IDSO 2024, it is recommended that participants have a basic understanding of data science principles, including statistics, data manipulation, and machine learning. Familiarity with programming languages such as Python or R is also advantageous. The competition is designed to be accessible to participants of varying skill levels, but those with prior experience in data science may find the challenges easier to tackle.

Will there be any mentorship or guidance provided during the competition?

Yes, IDSO 2024 offers mentorship opportunities for participants. Mentors are experienced data scientists and industry professionals who can provide guidance, feedback, and support throughout the competition. They can help you refine your approach, offer tips on best practices, and assist with any challenges you encounter. Mentorship sessions will be available through webinars, one-on-one meetings, and online forums.

Can I use pre-built models or libraries in my solution?

Yes, participants are allowed to use pre-built models and libraries to develop their solutions. However, it is important to clearly document and explain how these models or libraries are integrated into your solution. Additionally, originality and innovation in the approach are key judging criteria, so while using pre-built tools is acceptable, developing custom models or adding unique modifications can help your submission stand out.

What happens if I encounter a technical issue during the competition?

If you encounter any technical issues during the competition, such as problems with the submission platform, data access, or running your code, technical support will be available. You can reach out to the support team via the official competition platform or email. It is advised to report any issues as soon as possible to ensure that they can be resolved quickly, minimizing any impact on your participation.

NOTICE

The International Data Science Olympiad ("IDSOL") would like to address the spread of misinformation regarding IDSOL and IBCOL in Hong Kong SAR China. Please be cautious of individuals or organisations claiming to represent IDSOL without proper authorisation or documentation. This extends to unsubstantiated allegations against persons affiliated with IDSOL in Hong Kong SAR, China.

All participants of IDSOL are reminded that countries and territories with official organising committees will have the necessary documentation to authorise their representation. Participants must adhere to the processes set forth by the official committees to compete and qualify.

If you are unsure whether a website or communication you have received is legitimate, please contact us.
