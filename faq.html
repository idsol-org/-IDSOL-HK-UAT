General Questions

What is the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) 2024?

The International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) 2024 is a prestigious global competition that brings together talented data scientists, analysts, and enthusiasts from around the world to showcase their skills in data science, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Participants in IDSOL 2024 are challenged with complex problems requiring innovative data-driven solutions, allowing them to compete, collaborate, and demonstrate their expertise in a highly competitive environment. The competition is designed for both students and professionals, offering a platform to gain international recognition and contribute to the global data science community.

The IDSOL Finals is an annual event that brings hundreds of innovative students and professionals together. The mixing produces magical moments of inspiration and discussion, leading to further collaboration and possibly new projects and ventures.

What is the mission of IDSOL?

The mission of the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) is to foster the development of data science skills globally, promote innovation in the field, and create a collaborative environment where participants can solve real-world problems using data-driven approaches. IDSOL aims to identify and nurture emerging talents in data science, encourage knowledge sharing, and advance the global data science community by connecting individuals, institutions, and industries. Through this competition, IDSOL seeks to highlight the importance of data science in solving complex challenges across various sectors and contribute to the growth and accessibility of the field worldwide.

Are there any prerequisites to join the competition?

While there are no strict prerequisites for joining the IDSOL 2024, it is recommended that participants have a basic understanding of data science principles, including statistics, data manipulation, and machine learning. Familiarity with programming languages such as Python or R is also advantageous. The competition is designed to be accessible to participants of varying skill levels, but those with prior experience in data science may find the challenges easier to tackle.

Why I should join IDSOL?

Joining the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) offers a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in the rapidly evolving field of data science. Here’s why you should consider participating:

  • Global Recognition: IDSOL provides a prestigious platform where you can showcase your data science knowledge and skills on an international stage, gaining recognition from peers, industry leaders, and potential employers.
  • Skill Development: By competing in IDSOL, you'll tackle real-world challenges that require creative and innovative data-driven solutions, helping you sharpen your technical, analytical, and problem-solving skills.
  • Networking Opportunities: IDSOL connects you with a global community of data science enthusiasts, professionals, and experts. This network can open doors to collaborations, internships, job opportunities, and mentorship in the data science field.
  • Learning Experience: Whether you're a student or a professional, IDSOL offers a valuable learning experience through exposure to cutting-edge data science projects, workshops, and expert guidance, enhancing your understanding of this transformative field.
  • Boost Your Career: Competing in and potentially winning a global competition like IDSOL can significantly enhance your resume, making you stand out to employers in tech, finance, healthcare, and other industries that increasingly rely on data science expertise.

Joining IDSOL is more than just a competition—it’s an opportunity to grow, innovate, and become a part of the global data science revolution.

    What are the eligibility requirements for participants?

    The eligibility requirements for participants in the International Data Science Olympiad (IDSOL) generally include the following criteria:

    • Age Requirement: Participants are typically required to be at least 18 years old. However, there may be provisions for younger participants in specific categories or divisions, depending on the competition rules.
    • Educational Background: IDSOL is open to both students and professionals. Participants should have a background in data science, statistics, mathematics, computer science, or related fields. Students may be required to be enrolled in a recognized educational institution at the time of registration.
    • Team Composition: Participants can usually compete either individually or as part of a team. Teams typically consist of 2-5 members, and it’s encouraged to have a diverse mix of skills, such as data analysis, programming, and domain expertise.
    • Global Participation: IDSOL is an international competition, welcoming participants from all countries. There are generally no geographic restrictions, and participants from any nation can compete.
    • Experience Level: IDSOL is designed for participants of varying experience levels. While there are no strict restrictions based on prior experience, the competition may feature different divisions or tracks for beginners, intermediate, and advanced participants.
    • Registration: All participants must complete the registration process by the specified deadline, which may include providing necessary documentation, such as proof of age, educational status, or professional experience. Participants must also agree to the competition's terms and conditions.

    Should I join if I am not studying IT or ICT or STEM or...?

    Yes! Data Science is not only for “technology” students. In fact, if you’re from a tech background, we advise you to think beyond technology, and avoid the trap of creating a solution looking for a problem!

    Should I join if I am not studying business or commerce?

    Yes! Data Science is not only for tech or business students. Historically, students from a diverse range of majors or interests have competed on equal footing, as the solution design requires deep thinking into problem solving for specific domains and verticals.

    What is a proposal?

    A team of students (up to six per team) describes the problem or challenge being addressed, an overview of the solution, details of the solution architecture, and demonstrates how the solution addresses the problem. Elements of a business plan like market analysis, competitive analysis, revenue model, distribution strategy, etc are optional — i.e. do it if it helps explain your solution design. Likewise, elements of a project plan like development plan, testing plan, financial plan, etc are optional.

    What is the format of a proposal?

    Your proposal for a data science solution in IDSOL can be expressed using various tools and formats, depending on what best suits your project (Mention any sponsor tools if applicable). The following items must be included in your submission:

    • Flow diagram to illustrate the process or system architecture
    • High-fidelity prototype to demonstrate the functionality of your solution
    • Governance package outlining the rules and guidelines for managing the project
    • Poster board for visual communication of key ideas
    • Pitch deck to effectively present your solution to stakeholders

    While not mandatory, it is highly recommended that you create a recording of your pitch presentation and prepare a litepaper to summarize your project. The litepaper should be concise, not exceeding 4 pages (excluding appendices), and should follow the IEEE citation style for any references used. These additional components can help to enhance the clarity and impact of your proposal, providing a comprehensive view of your solution.

    What is the Flow Diagram?

    A flow diagram is a visual representation of the process or system architecture of your proposed data science solution. It maps out the steps involved in the solution, showing how data flows through the system, the interactions between different components, and the overall structure. This diagram helps to clarify the logic and functionality of your solution, making it easier for others to understand how it works.

    What is the High-Fidelity Prototype?

    A high-fidelity prototype is a detailed and interactive version of your solution, closely resembling the final product in terms of design and functionality. It is used to demonstrate how users would interact with your solution, providing a realistic preview of the user interface, features, and overall user experience. The prototype should effectively convey how your solution works and its potential impact.

    What is the Governance Package?

    A governance package outlines the rules, policies, and procedures that will guide the development, implementation, and maintenance of your data science solution. It includes details on decision-making processes, roles and responsibilities, data governance, and compliance with relevant regulations. The governance package ensures that the solution is managed effectively and aligns with ethical and legal standards.

    What is the Poster Board?

    A poster board is a large, flat, rectangular board used to visually display key information about your project in a clear and concise manner. It typically includes summaries of the problem, solution, and important data, presented in a visually appealing format that is easy to understand. The poster board is often used during presentations or exhibitions to quickly communicate the core aspects of your project.

    What is the Pitch Deck?

    A pitch deck is a visual presentation that provides an overview of your project idea. The purpose of a pitch deck is to clearly and concisely communicate the value proposition, market opportunity, and competitive advantages of your data science solution. It is designed to persuade stakeholders, such as judges, investors, or potential partners, of the viability and impact of your project.

    What Level of Technicality is Expected?

    The most important aspect of a data science solution is the design. A strong design is crucial for success, even if the implementation is not fully developed. While it is not required to implement a working prototype to demonstrate technical skills—meaning no coding is necessary—teams are welcome to include technical details or prototypes if they believe it enhances the explanation of their solution.

    How Many Proposals Are Allowed Per Team?

    Teams may pursue multiple project proposals and can enter multiple proposals for the exhibition event. However, each team is only allowed to compete with one proposal in the main competition.

    What is the Criteria for Team Formation?

    Teams may be composed of 1 to 6 eligible individuals. Each team must designate one member as the team lead. Individuals are not permitted to be part of more than one team.

    Could My Teammates Be from Different Schools?

    Yes, teammates can be from different schools. The representative school will be the one of the team leader. If a team includes both secondary and tertiary-level members, and at least one member is from a tertiary-level institution, the team will be classified as a tertiary-level team.

    What is the Eligibility of Individual Participants?

    Current and recent students (without age limit) may register as competitors. The Competitions Committee may request proof of enrollment or proof of graduation.

    Definitions:

    • Current Secondary-Level Students: Any current student studying at a secondary academic institution (ISCED 2011 level 2-4).
    • Current Tertiary-Level Students: Any current student studying at a tertiary academic institution (ISCED 2011 level 5-8).
    • Recent College or University Students: Any recent student of a tertiary academic institution within and including up to three (3) years since graduation or hiatus. The Competitions Committee may request an endorsement from academic or administrative staff from their institutions.

    Nominations

    How Are Teams Nominated for IDSOL 2024?

    There are three ways for a team to be nominated for IDSOL 2024:

    • An Officially-Sanctioned National Data Science Olympiad Committee: If a national data science olympiad is active this year in your country or territory, please refer to them directly for all details on their process. IDSOL 2024 receives competition and exhibition nominations from them directly, without additional secondary screening activities.
    • An Officially-Sanctioned National-Level Nominating Party: If a national data science olympiad is not active this year in your country or territory, you may be nominated by an organization within your country or territory. IDSOL 2024 receives competition and exhibition nominations from them directly, with possible secondary screening activities if deemed necessary by the Competitions Committee.
    • Self-Nomination: Teams from countries or territories without a National Committee (NC) or National Nominator (NN) that have eligible individuals may directly submit their proposal to the International Data Science Olympiad Committee and proceed to secondary screening.

    Who Can Nominate Teams for IDSOL 2024?

    There are three parties that can nominate teams:

    • National Committee (NC): A self-organized group of two or more National Nominators (NN) who are expected to host a competition for selection.
    • National Nominator (NN): Authorized by IDSOL 2024 to nominate teams to compete. NN are required to inform the IDSOL Competitions Committee of their nomination process.
    • Self-Nomination (SN): Teams from countries or territories without an NC or NN can submit their proposal directly to IDSOL and proceed through secondary screening.

    What Are National Nominators (NN)?

    National Nominators (NN) are organizations authorized by IDSOL 2024 to nominate teams for the competition. They are responsible for informing the IDSOL Competitions Committee about their nomination process. Secondary screening may be conducted for nominees from NN depending on the process and other factors specific to that country or territory.

    What Are National Committees (NC)?

    National Committees (NC) are self-organized by two or more National Nominators (NN). NCs are expected to host a competition to select teams for nomination. Teams nominated by NCs are directly submitted to IDSOL without the need for secondary screening.

    Can NN Coexist with an NC?

    Yes, NN and NC can coexist. However, if an NN does not collaborate with the NC, the nominees from that NN must provide a valid reason for not participating in the NC's processes and will undergo secondary screening. Generally, NN are encouraged to work with NC.

    How Many Teams Can Be Nominated Per Country or Territory?

    There is no limit to the number of nominations per country or territory.

    How Does Self-Nomination Work?

    Teams from countries or territories without an NC or NN that have eligible individuals may directly submit their proposal to the International Data Science Olympiad Committee and proceed to secondary screening.

    Can Teams Self-Nominate in Countries or Territories with NN or NC?

    No, unless the NC has rules stating otherwise.

    Where Can I See a List of NC or NN?

    A list will be provided. More information will be posted before the official start of IDSOL 2024.

    What Is the Policy for Extensions?

    Extensions must be requested at least 7 days before a deadline and will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis. Valid reasons for extensions may include, but are not limited to, war, civil unrest, natural disasters, and other force majeure events.

    What Is Secondary Screening?

    The International Data Science Olympiad Committee conducts secondary screening for nominees from countries or territories without an NC and for some nominees passed by NN under special circumstances. The evaluation is conducted using the standard criteria by a jury panel selected from a pool of global experts. If the nominee passes the evaluation, they are allowed to proceed.

    What Is a Nominee and a Finalist?

    A nominee becomes a finalist when the IDSOL 2024 Competitions Committee officially recognizes the team and enters them into the registry for IDSOL.

    Competition

    When is IDSOL 2024?

    IDSOL 2024 is scheduled to take place on November 8-10, 2024.

    Where is IDSOL 2024 being held?

    IDSOL 2024 will be hosted at inHolland University, located in Amsterdam, Netherlands. The university is providing state-of-the-art facilities for participants to showcase their data science solutions.

    Who may attend the competition?

    Students, educators, and members of the media are granted free admission to IDSOL 2024. Other attendees, including industry professionals and general public, may purchase tickets to attend the event.

    What are the prizes?

    Prizes will be awarded to the top-performing teams, including cash rewards, scholarships, and opportunities for internships or collaborations with leading data science companies. Additional sponsor-specific prizes will also be announced closer to the event date.
